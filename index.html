Thomas A. Edison

The Wizard of Menlo Park

This is a picture of Thomas Edison in 1922

Here is a timeline of Thomas Edison's life:

  • 1847 - Born in Milan, Ohio
  • 1854 - Edison's family moved to Port Huron, Michigan due to declining business
  • 1863 - Edison started work as a telegraph operator for the Grand Trunk Railway at Stratford, Ontario railway
  • 1866 - Edison moved to Louisville, Kentucky, where, as an employee of Western Union, he worked the Associated Press bureau news wire. Edison requested the night shift, which allowed him plenty of time to spend at his two favorite pastimes—reading and experimenting
  • 1869 - Edison's first patent - was for the electric vote recorder, U.S. Patent 90,646, which was granted on June 1
  • 1871 - Married Mary Stilwell (1855–1884), whom he had met two months earlier; she was an employee at one of his shops and they had three children together
  • 1876 - Edison set up his home and research laboratory in Menlo Park, which at the time was the site of an unsuccessful real estate development. While there, he earned the nickname "the Wizard of Menlo Park". The Menlo Park lab was significant in that was one of the first laboratories to pursue practical, commercial applications of research. It was in his Menlo Park laboratory that Thomas Edison invented both the phonograph and a commercially viable incandescent light bulb filament.
  • 1877 - Edison invented the phonograph, which gained him celebrity status
  • 1879 - Edison made the first successful test and public demonstration of the incandescent light bulb in Menlo Park.
  • 1884 - Mary Edison died on August 9, of unknown causes
  • 1886 - Married Mina Miller (1865–1947) in Akron, Ohio. They also had three children together
  • 1892 - Edison co-founded General Electric together with Charles A. Coffin, Elihu Thomson and Edwin J. Houston.
  • 1899 - Edison was awarded the Edward Longstreth Medal of The Franklin Institute.
  • 1908 - Edison received the American Association of Engineering Societies John Fritz Medal, for his outstanding scientific and industrial achievements.
  • 1915 - Edison was awarded Franklin Medal of The Franklin Institute for discoveries contributing to the foundation of industries and the well-being of the human race.
  • 1931 - Edison died at the age of 84 of complications of diabetes on October 18, 1931, in his home, "Glenmont" in Llewellyn Park in West Orange, New Jersey, which he had purchased in 1886 as a wedding gift for Mina. He is buried behind the home. Edison's last breath is reportedly contained in a test tube at The Henry Ford museum near Detroit.
